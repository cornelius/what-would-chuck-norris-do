Chuck Norris wouldn’t start with a rewrite. He’d hit the deadline first, then split what hurts most.

Pragmatic version for your team:
1. Keep the monolith for the 6‑month deadline.
2. Extract only 1 service now, and only if it removes a clear bottleneck (scaling, deploy risk, team ownership).
3. Define hard criteria for any further extraction (e.g., independent deploy need, load isolation, or regulatory boundary).
4. Reassess after launch with real usage data.

If you’re split, this is the least risky path: deliver product, reduce architecture religion, and let evidence decide.
