## Use PostgreSQL.

Here's why this is the right call:

**The "MongoDB is faster for documents" argument is outdated and wrong for your situation.**

PostgreSQL has had a mature, high-performance `JSONB` column type for years. You get native document storage with full indexing, querying, and flexibility — without sacrificing anything. It is not a compromise. It is genuinely good at document workloads.

**The real advantages that will matter to your startup:**

1. **Your team already knows it.** This is decisive. Expertise compounds. Onboarding, debugging, schema design, performance tuning — all faster. Two weeks of debate has already cost you more than the database choice ever will.
2. **ACID transactions are not optional.** As your product grows, you will hit a moment where you desperately need reliable transactions across multiple records. PostgreSQL gives you this for free. MongoDB's multi-document transactions are bolted on, limited, and a pain.
3. **Schema flexibility is still yours.** Use `JSONB` columns for the document-heavy parts. Use structured columns everywhere else. You get both worlds in one system.
4. **One fewer system to operate.** MongoDB adds operational complexity, backup strategies, a separate query language to maintain, and another tool for engineers to context-switch into.
5. **You can't predict your data model yet.** Startups pivot. PostgreSQL handles relational, document, time-series (TimescaleDB), and full-text search. MongoDB locks you into a document-first mental model that becomes a liability the moment your data gets relational.

**The only reason to choose MongoDB is if your team had deep MongoDB expertise and zero PostgreSQL experience.** That is the exact opposite of your situation.

**Decision: PostgreSQL. Start building.**
