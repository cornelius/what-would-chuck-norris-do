## 🔫 What Rambo Would Do (and What You Should Too)

Rambo would **take the shot he has right now** — then come back with the right weapons later.

### ✅ Step 1: Ship the Targeted Patch — TODAY

**Rambo doesn't let 5% of users suffer for 3 days while he regroups.** Here's why the patch wins *right now*:

- **Users are hurting in production.** Every hour matters.
- A 30-minute fix is **low risk, high speed, and reversible.**
- It buys you **breathing room** to do the refactor *properly*, without pressure.
- Rushed 3-day refactors under production-fire pressure are how you turn a 5% bug into a 50% outage.

### 📋 Step 2: Schedule the Refactor — Properly

But Rambo isn't reckless — he plans the next mission:

- **Document** why the patch is a band-aid and what the real fix is.
- **Open a ticket** immediately, before the urgency fades (it will).
- **Advocate for the sprint time** to do it right, now that you have evidence of the underlying problem.
- Add a **regression test** that the patch itself introduces, so the refactor has a safety net.

---

## The Golden Rule Here:

> **"Make it stop bleeding, then fix the wound properly."**

The real mistake isn't patching — it's **patching and forgetting.** The patch is only a bad decision if it never leads to the refactor.

So: patch now, ticket immediately, refactor soon. That's not cutting corners — that's professional triage. 🎯
