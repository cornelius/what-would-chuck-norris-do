## ✅ The Pragmatic Path: Modularize, Don't Migrate

You don't have to choose between "big ball of mud monolith" and "full microservices." There's a well-proven middle path:

1. **Ship the product.** The monolith got you here. Let it get you to launch.
2. **Draw bounded contexts *now*.** Identify the seams in your Rails app (e.g., billing, auth, notifications). Don't extract them — just *define* them clearly in code with clean module boundaries.
3. **Strangle the monolith *after* launch.** Use the [Strangler Fig pattern](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/StranglerFigApplication.html) to extract services incrementally, one at a time, with real production load data to guide you.

---

## ⚠️ Why Migrating Now Would Likely Be a Disaster

- Microservices introduce **distributed systems complexity** (network failures, data consistency, observability, service discovery) that your team is clearly not aligned on yet.
- You'd be solving **two hard problems at once**: building the product *and* re-platforming your infrastructure.
- 6 months is tight even for a focused team. A split team mid-migration almost guarantees a missed deadline.

---

## 🎯 The Real Question to Ask Your Team

> *"Are we blocked **right now** by the monolith, or are we solving a problem we imagine we'll have later?"*

If the answer is "we imagine," ship the monolith. If a specific, concrete pain point exists (e.g., one team deploys block all others, one service needs independent scaling *today*), then extract **that one service** — nothing more.
