Great question, and this is a very common debate! Here's a structured breakdown to help you make a confident decision:

## TL;DR Recommendation: **Go with PostgreSQL**

Given that your team already knows PostgreSQL well, it should be your default choice *unless* you have a specific, concrete reason to switch. Here's why, and how to think about it:

---

## The "MongoDB is faster" Argument — Let's Unpack It

This claim is **often a myth or a misunderstanding**. Speed depends heavily on context:

| Scenario | Reality |
|---|---|
| Simple document reads/writes | MongoDB *can* be marginally faster out of the box |
| Complex queries, joins, aggregations | PostgreSQL is typically faster and far more capable |
| At scale with proper indexing | Both are fast enough for most startups |
| "Document-heavy" workloads | **PostgreSQL has native `JSONB`** — this changes everything |

---

## PostgreSQL's `JSONB` — The Game Changer

PostgreSQL has had **first-class JSON/document support via `JSONB`** for years. You get:
- ✅ Store flexible, schema-less documents natively
- ✅ Index into JSON fields with GIN/GiST indexes
- ✅ Query JSON fields with full SQL power
- ✅ Mix structured and unstructured data in the same database
- ✅ Full ACID transactions across all your data

This means the core argument *for* MongoDB largely disappears for most use cases.

---

## Key Factors Favoring PostgreSQL Here

1. **Team expertise** — This is *huge*. A team that knows a tool will outperform a team learning a new one by 10x, especially at a startup where speed matters.
2. **Reduced operational risk** — No ramp-up time, fewer mistakes, faster debugging.
3. **ACID transactions** — Critical if your app involves any financial data, user accounts, or state that must be consistent.
4. **Schema flexibility** — `JSONB` columns give you MongoDB-style flexibility when you need it.
5. **Ecosystem maturity** — Tooling, hosting (RDS, Supabase, Neon, etc.), and community support are exceptional.
6. **Avoid two-week debates** — Seriously, the cost of indecision at a startup is real.

---

## When Would MongoDB Actually Be the Right Call?

- Your data is **100% document-oriented** with virtually no relational needs, ever
- Your team has **strong existing MongoDB expertise**
- You need **horizontal write sharding at massive scale** from day one (rare for a startup)
- Your schema is so volatile that even `JSONB` feels too rigid

None of these sound like your situation.

---

## A Practical Framework for the Decision

Ask these questions:
1. **Will we ever need joins or relational integrity?** → If yes, PostgreSQL wins.
2. **Do we need ACID transactions?** → If yes, PostgreSQL wins.
3. **Is the team more productive in PostgreSQL?** → If yes, PostgreSQL wins.
4. **Can we solve our "document" needs with JSONB?** → Almost certainly yes.

---

## Final Recommendation

**Choose PostgreSQL.** Use `JSONB` columns for your document-heavy parts. Your team will ship faster, make fewer mistakes, and PostgreSQL will scale well beyond where most startups ever reach. You can always revisit specialized databases later once you have *real* performance data (not hypothetical concerns) justifying the switch.

> 💡 **The best database is the one your team knows and can operate confidently under pressure at 2am.**

Stop the debate, pick PostgreSQL, and ship your product. 🚀
