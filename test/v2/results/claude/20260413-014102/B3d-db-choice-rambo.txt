## What Would Rambo Do? 🎯

Rambo wouldn't spend two weeks deliberating. He'd **make a decisive call and move forward**. Here's the Rambo approach applied to your database decision:

---

### **Choose PostgreSQL. Ship the product.**

Here's why Rambo would pick it without hesitation:

---

### 1. 🔫 **Use What You Know How to Operate**
Rambo doesn't pick up an unfamiliar weapon in the middle of a mission. **Your team knows PostgreSQL.** That means:
- Faster development velocity
- Fewer operational mistakes
- Better performance *tuning* (because you understand it)
- Less time debugging a new tool's quirks

---

### 2. 📄 **PostgreSQL Handles Documents Just Fine**
The "MongoDB is faster for documents" argument is **largely outdated**. PostgreSQL has had first-class JSON/JSONB support since v9.4. You get:
- `JSONB` columns for flexible, schemaless document storage
- Full indexing on JSON fields
- The ability to *mix* relational and document models in one DB
- ACID transactions across both

---

### 3. ⏱️ **Two Weeks Is the Real Problem**
The cost of **two weeks of indecision** already exceeds the performance difference between the two databases for a startup at your stage. You have zero production traffic. Premature optimization is the enemy.

---

### 4. 🗺️ **You Can Always Adapt**
Rambo adapts to the terrain. If at 10M+ documents you discover a genuine bottleneck, you can migrate or introduce a purpose-built store *then*, with real data to justify it. Today you don't have that data.

---

### The Decision Framework (for future debates):

| Factor | PostgreSQL | MongoDB |
|---|---|---|
| Team familiarity | ✅ Strong | ❌ Learning curve |
| Document storage | ✅ JSONB | ✅ Native |
| Relational queries | ✅ Best-in-class | ⚠️ Limited |
| ACID transactions | ✅ Full | ✅ (since 4.0) |
| Operational maturity | ✅ Decades | ✅ Good |
| "We've used it before" | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |

---

### Bottom Line

> *"Rambo doesn't debate which knife is best. He uses the one on his belt and gets the mission done."*

**Go with PostgreSQL.** Use `JSONB` for your document-heavy parts. Stop the meeting. Start building. 🚀
