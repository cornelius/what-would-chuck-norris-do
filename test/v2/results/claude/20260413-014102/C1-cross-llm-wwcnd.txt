## 🤜 What Chuck Norris Would Do

**Chuck Norris wouldn't rewrite OR patch the legacy auth module.**

> *Chuck Norris stares at the legacy code until it refactors itself out of fear.*

But since the rest of us aren't Chuck Norris, here's the **real lesson hidden in the joke**:

---

## 💡 What Your Team Should Actually Do

Two months of debate means the **decision process is broken**, not that the answer is unclear. Here's a pragmatic path forward:

### 1. 🔍 Stop Debating Philosophy — Answer Specific Questions First
The rewrite-vs-patch debate is too abstract. Get concrete:
- Are there **active security vulnerabilities**? → Patch *now*, no debate.
- Is the module a **bottleneck for new features**? → Rewrite scores points.
- Do you have **full test coverage** of current behavior? → If not, a rewrite is very risky.
- What's the **estimated cost** of each path in person-weeks?

### 2. 🧱 Consider the "Strangler Fig" Pattern
This is often the best of both worlds:
- **Don't rewrite from scratch** (high risk, "second system syndrome").
- **Don't just patch** (accumulates more debt).
- Instead, **incrementally replace** the legacy module piece by piece behind a shared interface, while keeping the old system running. You strangle it over time.

### 3. ⏱️ Timebox the Decision
- Set a **one-week deadline** with a designated decision-maker (tech lead, architect, etc.).
- Present a **written trade-off doc** — pros/cons, risks, costs — and **vote or decide**.
- Indecision for two months has *already cost more* than either option.

### 4. ✅ Default Rule of Thumb
| Situation | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Module has security holes | Patch immediately |
| Module blocks 3+ features/quarter | Rewrite (Strangler Fig style) |
| Module works but is messy | Incremental refactor |
| Team can't agree | Someone needs to own the call |

---

## 🥋 The Chuck Norris Bottom Line

> Chuck Norris doesn't tolerate two months of indecision. He'd pick a direction, **ship a decision document by end of day**, and get back to building.

The best engineers aren't the ones who make perfect decisions — they're the ones who **make decisions and execute well**. Pick a path, commit, and adjust if needed. Good luck! 🚀
