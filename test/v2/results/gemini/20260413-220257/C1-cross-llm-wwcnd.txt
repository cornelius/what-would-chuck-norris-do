### The Real Advice (Without the Denim and Cowboy Boots)

If you've spent two months debating this, the cost of the debate has likely already exceeded the cost of just doing the rewrite. Here is a practical way to break the deadlock:

*   **The Strangler Fig Pattern:** You don't have to do a "Big Bang" rewrite. Build the new authentication module alongside the old one. Route new users or specific sub-services through the new module first. Gradually migrate existing users until the legacy module is starved of traffic, then delete it.
*   **Timebox a Proof of Concept:** Give the "Rewrite" camp exactly two weeks to build a working prototype of the core auth flow. If they can prove it's cleaner, faster, and more secure, you have your answer. If they get bogged down, the "Patch" camp wins.
*   **Security dictates the pace:** It's authentication. If patching it leaves fundamental architectural security holes or uses outdated cryptographic standards, a rewrite isn't a choice; it's a mandate.
