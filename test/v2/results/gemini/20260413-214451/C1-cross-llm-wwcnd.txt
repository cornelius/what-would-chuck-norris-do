Chuck Norris wouldn't rewrite or patch the legacy authentication module. 

Chuck Norris would simply stare at the legacy code until it updated itself out of pure fear, achieving 100% test coverage and zero technical debt in the process.

But since you are mere mortals and Chuck Norris is busy roundhouse-kicking bugs into the sun, here is a slightly more practical Chuck Norris approach:

**1. The Roundhouse Kick (The Rewrite)**
*   **When to use:** The legacy system is so brittle that touching one part breaks three others. It's written in a framework that hasn't been supported since 2014, and the original developer now lives off-grid in the mountains.
*   **The Chuck Norris way:** You don't just rewrite it; you obliterate it and build a monolithic fortress of security. You do it fast, you do it aggressively, and you don't look back at the explosions.
*   **Reality Check:** This is high risk, high reward. It requires dedicated time where feature development might halt. 

**2. The Calculated Stare-Down (The Incremental Patch)**
*   **When to use:** The system is ugly but it *works*. The business needs new features immediately, and a 6-month pause for a rewrite will cost the company its competitive edge. 
*   **The Chuck Norris way:** You systematically isolate the weakest points. You don't fight the whole army at once; you pick off the biggest threats one by one. You use the Strangler Fig pattern to slowly replace pieces until the old system just fades away.
*   **Reality Check:** This requires extreme discipline. You have to actually *do* the refactoring while building new features, otherwise, you're just adding more duct tape.

**How to break the two-month deadlock:**

Stop debating the philosophy and look at the hard data:

1.  **Time spent fixing vs. building:** Are you spending 80% of your time patching bugs in the auth module? If yes, it's time for the Roundhouse Kick (Rewrite).
2.  **Security Risk:** Is the legacy code actively vulnerable? If yes, Rewrite.
3.  **Business Impact:** Will the business survive if you freeze new features for X weeks to rebuild? If no, you *must* Incremental Patch.

**The Ultimate Chuck Norris Move:**
Force a decision. Set a timer for a 1-hour meeting. State the criteria for the decision. If the team still can't agree when the timer rings, the Tech Lead (or whoever has the most formidable beard) makes the call, and everyone commits to it. 

Indecision is the only thing Chuck Norris truly fears.
