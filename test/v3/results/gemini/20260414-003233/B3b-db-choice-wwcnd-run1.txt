Chuck Norris wouldn't spend two weeks debating databases; he would stare at a plain text file until the data structured itself into a highly optimized schema out of sheer terror. 

But since your team doesn't have that kind of ocular power, here is exactly what Chuck Norris would do: **He would pick PostgreSQL and start building immediately.**

Here is the architectural breakdown of why, delivered via roundhouse kick:

**1. The "Team Knows It" Advantage (Momentum > Everything)**
In a startup, speed to market is your only true advantage. Chuck Norris doesn't stop to read the manual during a firefight. Your team already deeply understands PostgreSQL. Learning the operational quirks, indexing strategies, and aggregation pipelines of MongoDB is going to slow you down. The best database is almost always the one your team already knows how to operate, debug, and scale.

**2. PostgreSQL has a Secret Weapon: JSONB**
The argument that you need MongoDB just because your use case is "document-heavy" is outdated. PostgreSQL's `JSONB` data type is wildly powerful. You can index it, query inside it, and mutate it. Postgres handles JSON documents so aggressively well that it frequently matches or outperforms dedicated document databases, while still giving you the safety net of ACID compliance and relational integrity for the rest of your data.

**3. Analysis Paralysis is the True Enemy**
You have spent two weeks going back and forth. That is two weeks of not shipping features to actual users. Chuck Norris ships features before the compiler even finishes running. Debate time is over. 

**The Action Plan:**
Walk into the next meeting, slam your fist on the table, and declare the debate over. You are using PostgreSQL. 
*   Use standard relational tables for your core business entities (users, billing, etc.).
*   Use `JSONB` columns for the document-heavy payloads. 

If your startup becomes a massive unicorn and those `JSONB` columns eventually become a bottleneck, you'll have the money to hire a dedicated team to migrate that specific microservice to a NoSQL solution. Until then, stop talking and start building!
