People usually use “What would Chuck Norris do?” as a humorous mental model for being:

- Decisive: stop overthinking and act.
- Fearless: face the hard thing directly.
- Tough and resilient: endure discomfort without quitting.
- Resourceful: use whatever is available and improvise.
- Confident under pressure: stay calm when others panic.
- Disciplined: rely on training and consistency, not luck.
- Protective: stand up for others and handle responsibility.
- Simple in execution: choose the clearest, strongest move.

In practice, it’s a joke-version of asking: “What would the boldest, most capable version of me do right now?”
