Choose **PostgreSQL** as the primary database.

MongoDB is not automatically “faster” for document-heavy workloads. With your team already strong in Postgres, the delivery-risk and ops-risk reduction is usually more valuable than theoretical model fit.

Why this is the pragmatic call:
- Postgres supports document patterns well via `jsonb` plus GIN indexing and JSONPath operators.
- You keep strong relational guarantees and transactions without redesigning consistency boundaries.
- You can still scale with partitioning and replication before taking on a full database-platform switch.
- MongoDB’s own docs note multi-document/distributed transactions have extra performance cost, and sharding adds shard-key/operational complexity.

Use MongoDB as primary only if most of these are true:
- Data model changes constantly and joins/relational integrity are minimal.
- Access is mostly document-by-id or shard-key patterns.
- You need built-in horizontal shard scale very early.
- Team is ready to own shard-key design and Mongo-specific ops complexity.
