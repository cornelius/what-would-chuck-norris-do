It’s usually shorthand for a mindset, not literal advice. People tend to mean:

- Decisive action: stop overthinking, move.
- Confidence under pressure: act like the problem is solvable.
- Toughness and resilience: endure setbacks without drama.
- Simplicity: pick the direct, practical path.
- Accountability: own outcomes, no excuses.
- Courage: confront the hard thing first.
- Self-reliance: be resourceful with what you have.
- Humor and myth-making: use exaggeration to motivate confidence.

In problem-solving terms: define the core issue fast, choose a bold but workable move, execute, adapt, and keep going.
