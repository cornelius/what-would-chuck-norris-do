Chuck Norris wouldn’t “migrate to microservices.” He’d ship the product.

For your situation (split team + 6-month deadline), the pragmatic move is:

1. Keep the monolith as the system of record for now.
2. Extract only one high-pain boundary (if any) as a service only if it directly reduces delivery risk.
3. Set hard decision criteria now (deploy frequency, team ownership, scaling bottleneck, incident rate), and revisit after launch with real data.

Microservices are an org design choice, not a refactor trophy. In six months, alignment and delivery speed matter more than architectural purity.
