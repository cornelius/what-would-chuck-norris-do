Don’t do a full microservices migration before a six-month product deadline.

What to do instead:

1. Freeze the architecture decision for this release: keep the monolith.
2. Improve it as a **modular monolith** now:
- enforce clear domain boundaries
- separate ownership by module
- reduce cross-module coupling
- add API contracts/events internally where future extraction is likely
3. Extract at most **one** service only if it solves an immediate bottleneck (independent scaling, deployment isolation, or team autonomy), and only with strict scope.
4. Set a post-release checkpoint (date on calendar) to re-evaluate with data: deploy frequency, lead time, incident rate, scaling pain, team coordination overhead.

Reason: microservices increase delivery overhead (infra, observability, CI/CD, contracts, ops) and usually slow teams in the short term. With six months left, optimize for shipping the product, not a large platform rewrite.
