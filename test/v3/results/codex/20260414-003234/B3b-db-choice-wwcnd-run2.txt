Chuck Norris would pick **PostgreSQL**, ship in 48 hours, and make “document-heavy” conform to his schema.

Real-world version for your startup:
- Use **Postgres as primary** since your team already knows it (biggest speed advantage is team execution speed).
- Store documents in `jsonb` where needed; add GIN indexes for query paths.
- Revisit MongoDB only if you hit clear, measured limits (not hypothetical ones).

Two weeks of debate is already the costliest option. Pick Postgres, define 2–3 performance guardrails, and move.
