Choose **PostgreSQL as the primary database**.

Why this is the pragmatic call for your situation:
- Your team already has strong PostgreSQL expertise (execution risk and delivery speed matter more than theoretical peak throughput).
- PostgreSQL handles document-heavy workloads well with `jsonb` + GIN indexes for key/value and path queries.
- You keep relational guarantees, mature transactions, and flexibility to mix structured + document data in one system.
- MongoDB can be very strong for document models, but its own docs show important transaction/operational constraints you’ll need to manage in production.

When MongoDB should win instead:
- Your data/access patterns are overwhelmingly document-native, mostly single-document atomic ops, and you want Mongo-specific scaling/operational patterns from day one.
- You’ve benchmarked your exact workload and Mongo is materially better (not just assumed better).

Practical decision:
- Default to **PostgreSQL now**.
- Revisit in 4–8 weeks with a workload benchmark (same queries/writes, same indexes, same hardware). If Mongo clearly beats it on your real SLA/cost targets, add it deliberately for that bounded use case instead of replacing primary storage.
