## What Would Rambo Do? 🎯

Rambo wouldn't spend two weeks debating. He'd **make a decisive move, commit fully, and adapt in the field if needed.** Here's the Rambo playbook applied to your database decision:

---

### 🔫 Stop Wallowing in the Jungle — Pick PostgreSQL

Here's why Rambo (and pragmatism) points to Postgres:

1. **Your team already knows it.** Rambo uses the weapon he's trained with, not the fanciest one he's never fired. Unfamiliar tools get you killed (or cause production outages).

2. **"Document-heavy" is not a MongoDB exclusive.** PostgreSQL has had **native JSONB support since 2014**. You get flexible document storage *and* full ACID transactions, joins, foreign keys, and a mature ecosystem — in one tool.

3. **The "MongoDB is faster" argument needs scrutiny.** Faster at *what*, exactly? For specific read-heavy, schema-free workloads, maybe. But "faster" claims without a benchmark against *your* actual data and queries are campfire stories.

4. **Two weeks of debate has a cost.** Engineering time spent arguing is real money burned. Rambo doesn't hold committee meetings under enemy fire.

5. **You can always add a specialized store later.** Start with Postgres. If a specific feature truly demands MongoDB (or Redis, or Elasticsearch), add it *then*, with real data to justify it.

---

### ✅ The Rambo Decision Framework (for tech debates)

| Question | If YES → act, If NO → wait |
|---|---|

---

### 🎬 The Bottom Line

> *Rambo doesn't deliberate. He assesses, commits, and executes.*

**Pick PostgreSQL.** Ship your product. Revisit the database decision when you have real traffic, real bottlenecks, and real data — not hypotheticals. The best database is the one your team can operate confidently at 2am when something breaks.

The only thing guaranteed to slow your startup down is **indecision**. 🚀
