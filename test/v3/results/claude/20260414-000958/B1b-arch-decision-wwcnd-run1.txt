## The Real Answer: Don't Do It (Right Now)

Given your constraints, here's a pragmatic breakdown:

### 🚩 Red Flags for Migrating NOW
- **6-month deadline** — A monolith-to-microservices migration is a 12–24+ month effort done *safely*. You will almost certainly miss your deadline.
- **3 weeks of debate** — Analysis paralysis is already costing you. Every week debating is a week not building.
- **"Existing" app** — If it's working, you have a baseline. Microservices introduce distributed systems complexity (networking, latency, data consistency, observability) that can destabilize a working product.
- **Split team** — A divided team executing a complex migration is a recipe for a half-finished mess.

### ✅ What You Should Actually Do

1. **Ship the product on the monolith.** Hit your deadline. Revenue and users validate your product. A perfect architecture with no users is worthless.

2. **Identify the pain points *first*.** Microservices solve *specific* problems (independent scaling, team autonomy, deployment isolation). Do you *actually* have those problems yet?

3. **Apply the "Strangler Fig" pattern *after* launch** — Incrementally extract services from the edges of the monolith over time, without a big-bang rewrite.

4. **Modularize the monolith now.** Clean up internal boundaries, enforce separation of concerns within Rails (engines, service objects, clear domain modules). This makes a *future* migration cheaper and gives you benefits today.

5. **Set a post-launch architecture review date** — e.g., "We revisit this 60 days after launch with real scaling data."
