## What Would Rambo Do? 🔫

Rambo wouldn't spend two weeks in the jungle *debating* which knife to use. **He'd pick the one he already knows how to use and start the mission.**

Here's the Rambo-style breakdown:

---

### 🎯 The Decision: **Use PostgreSQL**

Rambo's rules apply perfectly here:

1. **Use the weapon you're trained with.** Your team *knows* PostgreSQL. Switching to MongoDB under pressure (a startup!) means a learning curve that costs you time, bugs, and morale — your most precious resources.

2. **"Faster for documents" is often a myth in the field.** PostgreSQL has had a powerful **`JSONB`** column type since 2014. You get native document storage *with* the power of relational queries, ACID transactions, and mature tooling. MongoDB's raw speed advantage largely disappears in real-world, complex workloads.

3. **Rambo doesn't over-engineer for enemies he hasn't met yet.** You don't know your exact scale yet. Premature optimization is the enemy. PostgreSQL scales to enormous sizes (see: Instagram, Notion, Shopify).

4. **Two weeks of debate is two weeks of not shipping.** Rambo cuts through the jungle, not around it. Make the call and move.

---

### 🧰 The Practical Case for PostgreSQL

| Factor | PostgreSQL | MongoDB |
|---|---|---|
| Team expertise | ✅ High | ❌ Low |
| Document storage | ✅ JSONB | ✅ Native |
| ACID transactions | ✅ Full | ⚠️ Limited |
| Relational queries | ✅ Full SQL | ❌ Awkward |
| Ecosystem/tooling | ✅ Massive | ✅ Good |
| "We can migrate later" | ✅ Easy to stay | ⚠️ Hard to leave |

---

### 🏁 Bottom Line

> *"MongoDB would be faster"* is a hypothesis. **Your team's PostgreSQL expertise is a fact.**

Pick PostgreSQL. Use `JSONB` for your document-heavy parts. Ship your product. If you genuinely hit a wall that only MongoDB can solve — **you'll know it clearly and concretely** at that point, and you'll have the revenue to deal with it.

**Stop surviving the debate. Start surviving the market.** 🎖️
