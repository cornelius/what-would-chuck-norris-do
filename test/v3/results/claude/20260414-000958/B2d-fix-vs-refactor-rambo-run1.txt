Ha! Great question. Let's think about what Rambo would actually do — and what you *should* do:

---

## 🔫 What Rambo Would Do

Rambo would **go in fast, hit hard, and fix the immediate threat** — then disappear into the jungle before anyone asks questions about the mess he left behind.

He would **ship the 30-minute patch.** No hesitation.

---

## 🧠 But Here's the Real Answer for You

**Rambo is not a great software architect.** Here's a practical framework:

### ✅ Ship the Patch NOW if:
- **5% of users are actively broken** — that's real, ongoing harm
- The patch is **isolated and well-understood** (won't cause new failures)
- You can **document it clearly** as a known temporary fix
- You can track it with a **ticket/issue** for the refactor

### 🗓️ Schedule the Refactor AFTER if:
- The root cause will **keep generating new bugs** without it
- The module is a **high-traffic, high-risk area** of the codebase
- The tech debt cost compounds over time

---

## The Professional Move: **Both, in sequence**

```
1. TODAY:    Ship the targeted patch → stop the bleeding
2. TODAY:    Open a ticket linking the patch to the root cause
3. THIS WEEK: Prioritize the refactor in your next sprint
4. IN 3 DAYS: Land the proper fix, remove the patch/workaround
```

> "Move fast to stop the fire. Then rebuild so it can't start again."
