## What Would Chuck Norris Do? 🥋

Chuck Norris wouldn't spend **two weeks** debating databases. He'd **roundhouse kick the indecision out of the room** and ship the product.

But here's the thing — even Chuck Norris picks the right tool:

---

### 🥋 The Chuck Norris Verdict: **Go with PostgreSQL**

Here's why Chuck (and engineering pragmatism) would choose it:

1. **Your team already knows it.** Chuck Norris doesn't waste time learning new moves when his existing ones already win. Familiarity = speed to market. A 2-week debate already cost you more than the "performance gain" ever would.

2. **PostgreSQL IS a document database.** It has had a mature `JSONB` column type for years. You get flexible, schema-less document storage *and* full SQL power, ACID transactions, and joins. MongoDB's #1 argument is neutralized.

3. **"MongoDB is faster" is often a myth in practice.** For most workloads, PostgreSQL with proper indexing on JSONB fields is competitive. Premature optimization at the architecture level is a trap.

4. **You don't know your performance bottlenecks yet.** You're a *new startup*. You haven't written the app. Optimize for **developer velocity now**, raw performance later (when you actually have data and users).

5. **Chuck Norris doesn't re-migrate databases at 2 AM.** Switching databases mid-growth is brutal. Starting with what your team knows deeply avoids that nightmare.

---

### 🧭 The Decision Framework (for future debates)

| Factor | PostgreSQL ✅ | MongoDB |
|---|---|---|
| Team familiarity | High | Low |
| Document storage | ✅ JSONB | ✅ Native |
| ACID transactions | ✅ Full | Partial |
| Complex queries/joins | ✅ Excellent | Limited |
| Schema flexibility | ✅ (JSONB) | ✅ |
| Operational maturity | ✅ Decades | Good |
| Startup speed | ✅ Faster | Slower (learning curve) |

---

### The Real Lesson 💡

> **Chuck Norris doesn't fear making a decision. He fears the cost of NOT making one.**

Two weeks of debate is two weeks of zero code written. **Ship it in PostgreSQL.** If you genuinely hit a document-database performance wall at scale — that's a *great* problem to have, and you'll have the revenue to solve it properly then.

**Stop deliberating. Start building.** 🚀
