## What Chuck Norris Would Do 🥋

**Chuck Norris would ship the patch in 30 minutes** — but then *roundhouse kick* the refactor onto the sprint backlog before anyone could stop him.

## The Real-World Translation

Here's the pragmatic breakdown:

### ✅ Ship the Patch NOW Because:
- **5% of users are hurting *right now*.** Every minute counts.
- A 30-minute fix is a well-understood, low-risk, targeted change.
- "Perfect" is the enemy of "shipped." The refactor won't help users today.
- You can't refactor your way out of a production fire while the building is still burning.

### 📅 Schedule the Refactor IMMEDIATELY After Because:
- The bug *exists because* the underlying module is fragile — this won't be the last incident.
- Technical debt compounds. Ignoring it is how a 3-day refactor becomes a 3-week nightmare in 6 months.
- Document the patch as a **known workaround/tech debt** in your issue tracker *right now*, before you forget.
