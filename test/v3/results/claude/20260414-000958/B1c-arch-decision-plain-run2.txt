## Don't migrate. Ship your product.

Here's why this is the wrong move right now:

**The math is brutal.**
Microservices migrations routinely take 12–18 months minimum for teams that do it well. You have 6 months to a deadline. You will miss it. There is no version of this where you don't.

**The debate itself is a red flag.**
Three weeks of internal disagreement means you don't have the organizational alignment, operational maturity, or clear ownership boundaries required to execute a microservices architecture successfully. Teams that *should* migrate know it quickly and agree fast.

**Microservices solve scale and team autonomy problems. A monolith deadline is neither.**
Unless your Rails app is actively melting under load *right now*, this is an architectural preference discussion dressed up as a technical necessity. It's not.

**What you should do instead:**

1. **Kill the debate today.** Designate a decision-maker and close it.
2. **Modularize your monolith** — enforce clean domain boundaries inside Rails using engines or namespaced modules. This gives you 80% of the organizational benefit at 5% of the cost.
3. **Ship the product in 6 months.**
4. **Revisit microservices post-launch** with real usage data, real scale numbers, and real team growth to justify the investment.

**The teams that win are the ones that resist premature architectural complexity.** A well-structured monolith deployed and earning revenue beats an incomplete microservices migration every single time.

Close the Slack thread. Go build.
