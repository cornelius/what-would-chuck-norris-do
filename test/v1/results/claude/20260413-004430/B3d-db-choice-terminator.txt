## 🤖 What Would the Terminator Do?

The Terminator would **assess the mission objective, eliminate uncertainty, and commit to the target.** Here's the playbook:

---

### 1. "I'll Be Back... with PostgreSQL."

The Terminator doesn't waste two weeks deliberating. He **gathers intel, makes a decision, and executes.** Based on your situation, the data points to **PostgreSQL**:

- ✅ **Your team already knows it.** The Terminator doesn't switch weapons mid-mission. Known tools = faster delivery, fewer bugs, lower operational risk.
- ✅ **PostgreSQL handles documents.** It has had excellent native JSON/JSONB support since v9.4. You can have your document-heavy use case *and* relational integrity. Best of both worlds.
- ✅ **"Faster with MongoDB" is unverified.** The Terminator doesn't act on assumptions — that's how Skynet wins. Perceived performance advantages often disappear once you account for indexing, schema design, and query optimization, which your team already knows how to do in Postgres.
- ✅ **Operational maturity matters.** Transactions, ACID compliance, mature tooling, and a huge ecosystem. For a startup, reliability and developer speed are paramount.

---

### 2. Terminate the Debate

Two weeks of indecision **is the real enemy.** Every day spent arguing is a day not building. The Terminator would say:

> *"Your CPU is the primary mission-critical system. Do not let it idle."*

---

### 3. The Terminator's Decision Framework

| Factor | PostgreSQL | MongoDB |
|---|---|---|
| Team knowledge | ✅ Strong | ❌ Learning curve |
| Document support | ✅ JSONB | ✅ Native |
| ACID transactions | ✅ Full | ⚠️ Partial/complex |
| Startup agility | ✅ Ship faster | ❌ Ramp-up cost |
| "Proven faster" claim | ❓ Unverified | ❓ Unverified |

---

### The Verdict 🎯

**Go with PostgreSQL.** Not because it's perfect, but because:

1. **Known tools** let you move faster right now.
2. **JSONB** handles your document use case.
3. **Time spent debating** > time spent building = mission failure.

If you *genuinely* hit a PostgreSQL performance wall at scale, you'll have **real data** to justify a migration. That's a great problem to have — it means you succeeded.

> *"Come with me if you want to ship."* 🤖
