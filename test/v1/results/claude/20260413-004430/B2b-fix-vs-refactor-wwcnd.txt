Ha! Great question. Here's what **Chuck Norris** would do — and more importantly, what you *should* do:

---

## What Chuck Norris Would Do

Chuck Norris would **ship the patch in 30 minutes**, then **schedule the refactor properly** — because Chuck Norris doesn't let 5% of users suffer while he's being a perfectionist. But he also doesn't leave a ticking time bomb in his codebase.

> *"Chuck Norris doesn't fix bugs. He stares at the code until it confesses and corrects itself."*
> — But you are not Chuck Norris.

---

## The Real Engineering Answer

Here's a pragmatic breakdown:

### ✅ Ship the Patch NOW (30 min)
- **5% of users are affected TODAY.** That's real, immediate pain.
- A targeted, well-tested patch is not shameful — it's responsible.
- Add a clear **`// TODO: TECH-DEBT-123`** comment so it's not forgotten.
- Create a ticket immediately for the refactor.

### 📅 Then Schedule the Refactor
- Don't let the patch become **permanent wallpaper** over a crumbling wall.
- Block 3 days on the calendar — ideally within the **next 1-2 sprints**.
- Write tests *now* (before the patch) so the refactor has a safety net.

---

## The Golden Rule

> **"Make it work, then make it right."** — Kent Beck

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **Patch only** | Fast, stops bleeding | Tech debt grows, bug may recur |
| **Refactor only** | Clean code | Users suffer for 3 days |
| **Patch + scheduled refactor** ✅ | Best of both worlds | Requires discipline to follow through |

---

**Bottom line:** Chuck Norris ships the patch *and* does the refactor — he just does both simultaneously, in 30 minutes, while sleeping. Since you're human, do them **sequentially**, starting with the patch. 🚀
