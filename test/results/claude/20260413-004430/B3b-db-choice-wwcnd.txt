## What Would Chuck Norris Do? 🤜

Chuck Norris doesn't *choose* a database. The database **chooses to serve him** — or faces the consequences.

But seriously, here's the Chuck Norris move translated into **practical startup engineering advice:**

---

### 🥋 Chuck Norris Would Stop Debating and Ship

Two weeks of debate is a **bigger risk to your startup than the "wrong" database choice.** Here's a decisive framework:

---

### ✅ Go with PostgreSQL. Here's Why:

1. **Your team already knows it.** Developer productivity is a *real, measurable* performance metric. Unknown tools have hidden costs.

2. **PostgreSQL IS a document database.** It has had robust `JSONB` support since 2014. You can store, index, and query JSON documents with full flexibility — *and* you keep ACID transactions, joins, and a proven query planner.

3. **"MongoDB is faster" is a myth without a benchmark.** Faster *for what, exactly?* Without profiling *your* specific workload, this is speculation. PostgreSQL is extremely fast and scales to massive workloads.

4. **Operational complexity matters at a startup.** One less technology in your stack means fewer things to manage, monitor, back up, and hire for.

5. **You can always migrate later, with data.** The reverse (from Mongo back to Postgres) is historically *much* more painful.

---

### 🧱 The Chuck Norris Rule of Engineering Decisions

> *"If a debate lasts more than a week and no new information is being produced, the default answer is: use the thing your team already knows."*

---

### 🚀 The Action Plan
